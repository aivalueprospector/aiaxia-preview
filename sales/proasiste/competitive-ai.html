ProAsiste vs. ChatGPT / Claude Code / NotebookLM

The three AI-native tools prospects name most often. All three are good at what they do. None of them is what ProAsiste is: a private, permanent knowledge system that learns your business and keeps that knowledge for the whole team. Position complementary where it's honest, and lead with the gap each one leaves.

The one-line frame: "Those are general AI tools you point at a task. ProAsiste is a knowledge system that remembers your business — every supplier, contract, and decision — and answers from your data, not the internet's."

vs. ChatGPT

What It Is

A general-purpose chat assistant trained on the public internet. Free and paid tiers. The default tool a prospect already has open in a browser tab.

Their Argument

"My team already uses ChatGPT. Why would I pay for ProAsiste?"

Your Response

"ChatGPT knows the internet. ProAsiste knows YOUR business. Ask ChatGPT about your supplier terms and it gives you generic advice — because it has never seen your contracts. Ask ProAsiste and it gives you your actual terms, from your actual documents, and flags when a new one looks off compared to the rest. ChatGPT forgets the conversation the moment you close the tab; ProAsiste builds a permanent knowledge graph that gets smarter every month. And with ChatGPT, what your team types in can be used to train someone else's model. With ProAsiste, your data stays private to you — always."

vs. Claude Code

What It Is

Anthropic's agentic AI that works inside a software codebase from the terminal — it reads files, edits code, runs commands. A genuinely excellent tool. Built for developers.

Their Argument

"We have technical people using Claude Code. Can't they just point it at our business files?"

Your Response

"Claude Code is one of the best tools out there — for writing software. It lives in a codebase and a terminal, and it assumes the person driving it is an engineer. That's a different job from running your business knowledge. ProAsiste isn't a coding agent you have to operate; it's a finished knowledge system your whole team uses in a browser. It builds the knowledge graph for you, keeps a private wall between the PII your owner sees and the obfuscated version your team shares, and it's there for the salesperson and the office manager — not just the one engineer who knows the command line. Use Claude Code to build your product. Use ProAsiste to run the knowledge behind it."

vs. NotebookLM

What It Is

Google's research assistant: upload a set of sources into a "notebook" and ask questions grounded in those documents, with citations. Strong at summarizing a fixed pile of files.

Their Argument

"NotebookLM already lets me upload our documents and ask questions. Isn't that the same thing?"

Your Response

"NotebookLM is great for one project: drop in a stack of documents and ask about them. The catch is it stops at the edge of that notebook. It doesn't connect what's in notebook A to notebook B, it doesn't build a structured map of your suppliers and customers and the events between them, it doesn't automate anything, and it doesn't keep a private/public privacy wall for a team. ProAsiste isn't a notebook you fill and empty — it's a living graph of your whole business that grows month over month, links every document to every other, and lets you ask questions across all of it. NotebookLM answers 'what's in these files?' ProAsiste answers 'what does my business know, and what changed?'"

Competitive Landmines to Avoid

Don't Trash the Tool

  • ❌ "ChatGPT is useless for business."
  • ✓ "ChatGPT is great for general questions. ProAsiste is built to know your business."

Don't Misframe Claude Code

  • ❌ "Claude Code is a worse version of us."
  • ✓ "Claude Code is a brilliant developer tool. ProAsiste is for running business knowledge — different job, different user."

Don't Get Into Feature Wars

  • ❌ "We have more features than NotebookLM."
  • ✓ "The question is whether you want answers about one notebook or about your whole business over time."

Lead With Privacy When It's Live

  • The private-PII boundary and "never trained on your data" are the cleanest, least-debatable wins against ChatGPT and NotebookLM. Use them.
