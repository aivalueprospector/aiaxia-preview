Your data, and why more of it makes the Twin smarter

A Twin turns the documents, spreadsheets and records a business already has into connected knowledge it can answer questions from. But a Twin is only as smart as the data it has seen. It works with four kinds of data, each answering a different question, and it gets more useful the more you give it.

The one-line frame: "Your business already produces all of this data every day. It's just scattered across files and people's heads. The Twin gathers it into one connected picture, and the more you give it, the more it can tell you."

The four kinds of data

Data comes at different scales, and the Twin uses all four together: the big stable picture, the day-to-day activity, the web that ties them together, and the goals you're driving toward.

1Macro-data: the entities

The stable map of your business: every customer, supplier, product, contract and person. It changes slowly and forms the frame everything else hangs on. On its own it answers "who and what do we deal with?" It's your roster of the world your business operates in.

2Event-data: the timeline

Every meaningful thing that happens, time-stamped: an order placed, a payment received, a price increase, a delivery date met or missed. Events accumulate fastest and carry the signal of how the business moves. They answer "what happened, when, and how often?" Once you have months of them, they also answer "is this getting better or worse?"

3Micro-data: connections & causes

The threads between entities and between events: which supplier is tied to which product, which customer to which contract, and which event caused another. This is the layer that turns separate facts into something you can reason about. It answers "how is everything related, and what drives what?" For example, "late shipments from this supplier are what's driving our overtime cost."

4Outcome-data: goals & progress

The targets you care about, with a number attached and tracked over time. As events flow in, progress updates on its own. This answers the question that matters most to an owner: "are we getting closer to the goal, and by how much?"

What more data unlocks

Each kind of data is useful alone. Together, and at volume, they compound. More data isn't only about storage. It lets the Twin answer questions it couldn't before.

Give the Twin more……and you unlock
More entities + eventsA denser picture. Isolated facts become a connected web. The Twin can surface your most-connected, most-active relationships, the parts of the business that matter most.
More coverageVisible gaps. With more data in, the Twin can point out what's missing or stale: a customer gone quiet, a supplier never linked to a product, an area nothing has moved in for months.
More events over timeCause and effect. Enough history lets the Twin link causes to outcomes, like a late shipment driving a downstream cost, and answer in seconds what used to take days of cross-referencing.
More historySharper goals. A richer record makes progress tracking accurate and automatic, instead of a spreadsheet someone updates by hand.

10 documents vs. 50. With ten documents, the Twin answers questions about those ten. With fifty, it starts connecting them: the supplier in one file is now linked to the purchase orders, the delivery dates, and the cost overruns in the others, and you can ask "which suppliers raised prices more than 10% last quarter, and what did it cost us?" and get an answer from your own data. That gap only widens the more you feed it.
