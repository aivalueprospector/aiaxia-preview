When's the last time you knew, without a meeting, what every person on your team was working on?

The problem with running on status meetings

  • Status meetings to find out what already happened
  • Knowledge lives in people's heads, and walks out the door when they leave
  • Reports compiled by hand, stale the moment they're done
  • You're always one step behind your own operation

Speaker note: Nod along with the pain. Owners live this every day. If {{Pain Point}} came up on the discovery call, name it: "you told me {{Pain Point}}, that's exactly slide two." The "walks out the door" line hits hardest with anyone who's lost a key employee.

What if every person on your team had their own AI twin, and you had an owner twin too?

  • Team twins and your owner twin, talking to each other
  • Two twins. One continuous conversation.
  • This is ProAsiste: a daily standup that writes itself.

Speaker note: This is the aha. Pause here. It's a different mental model than project management. This isn't another tool your team has to update, it's a twin that already knows. Let it land before moving on.

See. Delegate.

  • See what your team handled today. Ask your twin "Where do the Q3 installs stand?" Live answer, pulled from your team's twins. No status meeting.
  • Delegate in one sentence. Type "have Maria follow up on the Henderson quote by Friday" and it lands in her twin's chat, tracked back to yours.

Speaker note: These are the two features that close the sale. Demo them live if you can. The live answer to "where do things stand" is the moment they lean in. For owners holding too much in their head and on sticky notes, the one-sentence delegation sells itself.

Onboard. Move goals.

  • Day-one context for new hires: every conversation, fact, and filed doc feeds a shared knowledge graph; new hires get years of context on day one without asking anyone.
  • Goals the whole team moves: set "$75K recurring by Q4," every team member's progress rolls up into the bar automatically.

Speaker note: This is where it stops looking like a chat tool and starts looking like an operating system for the business. The new-hire angle wins anyone who's lost institutional memory. The goal roll-up wins owners running structured, accountable quarters.

Oversight your team actually trusts

  • Administrator, employee, and mentor roles per seat
  • Everyone sees who can see what, with no black-box surveillance
  • Mentor seats are free
  • You get the full picture; your team keeps their trust

Speaker note: This converts owners who worry their team will see twins as spyware. Visibility with transparency is the whole pitch: you get oversight, they get to see it's not secret. Don't skip it for cautious teams.

  • Approval workflows for quotes and spend
