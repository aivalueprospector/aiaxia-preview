How Two Twins Work Together — Slide Outline

Audience: parents, tutors, small-school owners, homeschool co-ops Length: 8 slides, ~7 minutes Goal: get to the demo, then get to the ask

Slide 1 — The Hook

Title: When's the last time you really knew what your child learned today?

  • Large image: parent looking at phone, child doing homework in background
  • Subtitle: "Not what grade they got. What they learned."
  • No bullets. Let the question sit.

Speaker note: Don't answer the question. Wait 3 seconds. Let the room feel it.

Slide 2 — The Gap

Title: The problem with every learning app you've tried

  • Report cards come weeks late
  • Parent dashboards show scores, not understanding
  • Homework apps add friction, not signal
  • You're always one conversation behind your child

Speaker note: Nod along with the pain. Everyone in the room has lived this.

Slide 3 — The Shift

Title: What if your child had their own AI tutor — and you had one too?

  • Simple diagram: child twin ↔︎ parent twin, two circles with a bidirectional arrow
  • Subtitle: "Two twins. One continuous conversation."
  • Tagline at bottom: "This is TwinCloud."

Speaker note: This is the "aha." Pause here. It's a different mental model than ed-tech.

Slide 4 — Feature Pillar 1 + 2

Title: See. Assign.

  • See what your child learned today — Ask your twin anything: "How's Emma doing in reading?" Live answer.
  • Assign homework in 30 seconds — Type the topic. System builds the worksheet and a private answer key for you.

Speaker note: These are the two features that close the sale. Demo them live if possible.

Slide 5 — Feature Pillar 3 + 4

Title: Guide. Track.

  • Socratic study sessions — Your child's twin asks questions, doesn't give answers. You get a summary of what clicked when they're done.
  • Goals that actually move — Set it once. Progress updates itself as your child works.

Speaker note: This is where it stops looking like a worksheet app and starts looking like a tutor.

Slide 6 — Feature Pillar 5

Title: Built on trust

  • Every time you look at your child's data, they can see it happened
  • No secret surveillance
  • Your child's twin belongs to them — not to us, not to their school
  • Privacy is a feature, not a footnote

Speaker note: This slide converts privacy-skeptical parents. Don't skip it.

Slide 7 — What's Coming

Title: This month and next

  • Quizzes and tests with auto-grading
  • Classroom view — teachers managing whole cohorts
  • Co-parent collaboration — draft assignments together, send as a team

Speaker note: Shows momentum. Creates FOMO for early adopters.

Slide 8 — The Ask

Title: Start with one child this week

  • Free 30-day trial (Individual tier, no credit card)
  • 5-minute setup: you get a parent twin, your child gets a learning twin
  • First worksheet before dinner tonight

CTA button: Start free at ctwin.ai

Speaker note: Make it sound like a weekend experiment, not a year commitment. The trial sells itself once they see the first auto-summary.
