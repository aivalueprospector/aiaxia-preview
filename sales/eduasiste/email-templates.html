Best Practices

Tokens

Everything in {{double braces}} is a fill-in. Replace every one before sending, and double-check that no {{...}} slips through to the inbox. The common tokens:

  • {{Prospect Name}} — the recipient (the parent, tutor, or administrator)
  • {{Child Name}} — the learner; swap the example subject/child for a real one
  • {{Company}} — the school or tutoring business
  • {{Referrer}} · {{Pain Point}} — for warm intros; name a real referrer and a real, specific pain point in their own words, or the email reads like a mail merge
  • {{Reference}} — a comparable family, tutor, or school you can name
  • {{Price}} · {{Tier}} · {{Seats}} — current plan figures; fill from the live price sheet rather than quoting from memory
  • {{Date}} · {{Demo Link}} · {{Rep Name}} · {{Rep Email}}

Subject lines

  • Keep under 50 characters
  • Make it specific to their child, students, or school
  • Avoid spam triggers (FREE, LIMITED TIME, ACT NOW)
  • Questions often outperform statements

Body

  • Keep first email under 150 words
  • One clear call to action
  • Make it easy to say yes or no
  • Personalize with the child's name, the tutoring business, or the school
  • Lead with empathy, parent-to-parent and educator-to-educator

Timing

  • Tuesday–Thursday typically best
  • Early evening often works for parents (after pickup, before bedtime)
  • Avoid Mondays (inbox overload) and Fridays (weekend mode)

Follow-up cadence

  • Day 1: Initial email
  • Day 3: Follow-up if no response
  • Day 7: Different angle / value prop
  • Day 14: Final attempt
  • Then: Move to nurture sequence
