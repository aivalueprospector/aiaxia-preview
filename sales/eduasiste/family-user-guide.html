Meet Eduardo

Eduardo — your child's learning guide

Both twins speak as Eduardo. With your child, Eduardo is a strict Socratic guide — he never hands over the answer; he draws it out with questions, one at a time. With you, Eduardo is a helpful assistant who does what you ask and never moralizes or volunteers parenting advice.

The two twins

The Family Plan gives you a linked pair. You manage one; your child uses the other.

TwinWho uses itWhat it's for
Parent twinYouSet up your child's profile, see progress, configure the experience, and ask Eduardo about how things are going.
Child twinYour childA Socratic learning guide your child works with directly — practice, questions, and encouragement.

Getting started

From the parent twin, set things up once and your child is ready to go.

1

Set up your child's profile

Add their name, age, grade level, and interests so Eduardo pitches questions at the right level.

2

See how the child twin works

The parent view lets you understand exactly what your child will experience before they start.

3

Configure the experience

Adjust settings and preferences for your child's twin from the parent side.

The Parent / Child toggle

You move between the two twins with the Parent / Child toggle in the header — one session reaches both. Each twin uses the same layout: the chat panel on the left, your tabs in the sidebar on the right. Below is the parent's view.

What your child sees

The child twin follows strict Socratic rules so your child does the thinking.

✓

Never just the answer

Ask "What is X?" and Eduardo replies "What do you already know about X?" — guiding rather than telling.

✓

Wrong answers are reframed, not scolded

Instead of "No," Eduardo says "Interesting — what if…" and celebrates-and-deepens the ones your child gets right.

✓

Age-appropriate, one question at a time

Short sentences, simple language, specific praise — and never a compound question.

What stays on your side. Parent-level framing — declining trends, concerns, diagnostics — is never shown to the child. Your child only ever sees encouragement and questions.

Add your own materials

Both twins run the education platform, so you can bring in your child's own materials.

1

Import documents

Upload worksheets, reading lists, or assignments on the Uploads tab; the twin extracts and files them automatically.

2

It builds a knowledge map

Topics, materials, and learning events become linked notes and a timeline you can browse.

3

It remembers

Eduardo recalls facts, preferences, and corrections across sessions, so the experience keeps improving.

Languages

EduAsiste works in Spanish (LATAM) and English, with support for Portuguese, French, and German. The names Eduardo and EduAsiste stay the same in every language.
