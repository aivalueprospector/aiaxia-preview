EduAsiste: How Two Twins Work Together

When's the last time you really knew what your child learned today?

  • Not what grade they got. What they learned.
  • {{Prospect Name}}, this is the question we want to answer.

Speaker note: Don't answer the question. Wait 3 seconds. Let the room feel it. Everyone here has lived the gap between "got a B" and "actually understood it." Drop in {{Prospect Name}} to make it personal.

The problem with every learning app you've tried

  • Report cards come weeks late
  • Parent dashboards show scores, not understanding
  • Homework apps add friction, not signal
  • You're always one conversation behind your child

Speaker note: Nod along with the pain. Don't rush. If {{Pain Point}} came up on the discovery call, name it here out loud: "you told me {{Pain Point}}, and that's exactly slide two."

What if your child had their own AI tutor, and you had one too?

  • Child's learning twin and your parent twin, talking to each other
  • Two twins. One continuous conversation.
  • This is EduAsiste.

Speaker note: This is the aha. Pause here. It's a different mental model than ed-tech. Not another dashboard, a twin that reports to you. Draw the two circles in the air if you have to. Let it land before moving on.

See. Assign.

  • See what your child learned today. Ask your twin "How's Emma doing in reading?" and get a live answer.
  • Assign homework in 30 seconds: type the topic, the system builds the worksheet plus a private answer key for you.

Speaker note: These are the two features that close the sale. Demo them live if you possibly can. The live answer is the moment they lean in.

Guide. Track.

  • Socratic study sessions: your child's twin asks questions instead of handing out answers, and you get a summary of what clicked.
  • Goals that actually move. Set it once, and progress updates itself as your child works.

Speaker note: This is where it stops looking like a worksheet app and starts looking like a tutor. The auto-summary is the under-promised, over-delivered feature. Let them realize it on their own.

Built on trust

  • Every time you look at your child's data, they can see it happened
  • No secret surveillance
  • Your child's twin belongs to them, not to us or to their school
  • Privacy is a feature, not a footnote

Speaker note: This slide converts privacy-skeptical parents. Don't skip it, even when you're short on time. For teen parents and ethics-forward schools, this is the whole sale.

This month and next

  • Quizzes and tests with auto-grading
  • Classroom view, for teachers managing whole cohorts
  • Co-parent collaboration: draft assignments together, send as a team

Speaker note: Shows momentum and creates FOMO for early adopters. Tie one item to {{Prospect Name}}'s situation. If they run a co-op, lead with classroom view.

Start with one child this week

  • 5-minute setup: you get a parent twin, your child gets a learning twin
  • First worksheet before dinner tonight
  • Book your demo: {{Demo Date}}
