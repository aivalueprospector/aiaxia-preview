The EduAsiste twin

What the education twin does, then how to use it. EduAsiste is a linked pair: a learner twin the child works with directly, and an adult twin — a parent on the Family Plan, a tutor on the Mentor Plan — that stays in the loop. Learner + adult, one pair

The adult sets up the learner's profile and monitors progress; the child talks to Eduardo directly. Both speak as Eduardo — one voice, two views.

Socratic guide

Eduardo never hands over the answer. He reframes wrong answers, deepens correct ones, and asks one question at a time — the child does the work.

Full adult visibility

Read every conversation, ask Eduardo yourself, or flip to the child's view with the header toggle. Child-level diagnostics never surface to the child.

Progress tracking

Streaks, goals, and recent learning events surface to the adult, with a weekly briefing — so you see momentum and stumbles without babysitting.

Knowledge capture + graph

Upload documents and Eduardo extracts them into entity notes and a knowledge graph (Neo4j + Postgres), aligned to your state's curriculum.

Bilingual

Works in English and Spanish — the same twin, ready for bilingual families and students switching between the two.

Meet Eduardo

Eduardo — one voice on both sides of the table

Both twins speak as Eduardo. With the learner, Eduardo is a strict Socratic guide — he never hands over the answer; he draws it out with questions, one at a time, scoped to the subject. With the adult — a parent on the Family Plan, a tutor on the Mentor Plan — Eduardo is a collegial, helpful assistant who does what you ask, never moralizes, and never volunteers parenting advice.

The two plans, the two twins

EduAsiste comes as a linked pair in two flavors. The Family Plan pairs a parent with a child; the Mentor Plan pairs a tutor with a mentee and turns a once-a-week session into continuous async support.

PlanAdult twinLearner twin
Family PlanParent twin — set up the child's profile, see progress, configure the experience, and ask Eduardo how things are going.Child twin — a Socratic learning guide the child works with directly: practice, questions, and encouragement.
Mentor PlanMentor twin — document curriculum, track the mentee, generate reports and parent recaps, and read what the mentee has been working on (read-only).Mentee twin — a Socratic guide for between-session practice, scoped to the subject you mentor.

Getting started

For Families — set up your child

From the parent twin, set things up once and your child is ready to go.

1

Set up your child's profile

Add their name, age, grade level, and interests so Eduardo pitches questions at the right level.

2

See how the child twin works

The parent view lets you understand exactly what your child will experience before they start.

3

Configure the experience

Adjust settings and preferences for your child's twin from the parent side.

For Mentors — run your practice

The mentor twin is your workspace. Here is what you do in it day to day.

1

Document curriculum & sessions

Load lesson plans, session plans, and assessments. Eduardo files them and aligns activities to curriculum standards.

2

Track your mentee

Progress shows up as events and goals — what they practiced, where they got stuck, what they've mastered.

3

Generate reports & parent recaps

Produce a Progress Report, Session Summary, or Attendance Log, and send a short recap to your mentee's parent after each session.

The mentor twin has read-only access to the mentee's workspace — between-session practice, stuck flags, and small wins. Your mentee cannot see into your workspace; the access only runs one way.

What the learner sees

Whether it's a child on the Family Plan or a mentee on the Mentor Plan, the learner twin follows strict Socratic rules so the student does the thinking.

✓

Never just the answer

Ask "What is X?" and Eduardo replies "What do you already know about X?" — guiding rather than telling.

✓

Wrong answers are reframed, not scolded

Instead of "No," Eduardo says "Interesting — what if…" and celebrates-and-deepens the ones the learner gets right.

✓

Age-appropriate, one question at a time

Short sentences, simple language, specific praise — and never a compound question.

✓

Between-session practice, stuck flags & a wins journal

The learner keeps working when the adult isn't there. When they get stuck it's flagged for you; small wins are journaled so you can build on them next time.

What stays on the adult's side. Adult-level framing — declining trends, concerns, diagnostics — is never shown to the learner. The child or mentee only ever sees encouragement and questions.

Progress & reports

For Families — track progress

From the parent twin you can see how your child is doing and read every exchange.

WhatWhat it shows
StreaksHow many days running your child has practiced.
GoalsLearning objectives and progress toward them.
Recent eventsA timeline of what your child worked on and where they got stuck.
Every conversationYou can read every exchange, and ask Eduardo about it yourself.

For Mentors — reports & recaps

Reports are template-driven and two-phase: Eduardo finds a matching template, shows you a plan, and only generates after you approve.

ReportWhat it covers
Progress ReportAn individual mentee's events over a period.
Session / Class SummaryScores by assessment and their distribution.
Attendance LogA record of sessions attended.
Parent recapA short progress summary sent to the mentee's parent after each session.
Output is generated as PDF, Excel, or Word and saved to the Reports tab. Reports you run often can be saved as Tasks and re-run later.

Add your own materials

Both twins run the education platform, so you can bring in the learner's own materials — worksheets and reading lists for a family, curriculum and assessments for a mentor.

1

Import documents

Upload worksheets, reading lists, curriculum, or assessments on the Uploads tab; the twin extracts and files them automatically.

2

It builds a knowledge map

Topics, materials, sessions, and learning events become linked notes, a timeline, and goals you can track.

3

It remembers

Eduardo recalls facts, preferences, and corrections across sessions, so the experience keeps improving.

Languages

EduAsiste works in Spanish (LATAM) and English, with support for Portuguese, French, and German. The names Eduardo and EduAsiste stay the same in every language — only the explanatory text is localized.
