What the EduAsiste twin does

A quick tour of the education twin: a linked parent twin and child twin, both speaking as Eduardo. The child works through every problem; the parent sees every exchange.

← EduAsiste materials

The shape of it

One linked pair: a parent twin to set up and monitor, and a child twin the kid talks to directly. Eduardo is a strict Socratic guide — he never hands over the answer. A Parent/Child toggle in the header flips between the two views. Below: roughly what each side sees.

Features

Parent + child, one pair

The parent sets up the child's profile and monitors progress; the child talks to Eduardo directly. Both speak as Eduardo — one voice, two views.

Socratic guide

Eduardo never hands over the answer. He reframes wrong answers, deepens correct ones, and asks one question at a time — the child does the work.

Full parent visibility

Read every conversation, ask Eduardo yourself, or flip to the child's view with the header toggle. Child-level diagnostics never surface to the child.

Progress tracking

Streaks, goals, and recent learning events surface to the parent, with a weekly briefing — so you see momentum and stumbles without babysitting.

Knowledge capture + graph

Upload documents and Eduardo extracts them into entity notes and a knowledge graph (Neo4j + Postgres), aligned to your state's curriculum.

Bilingual

Works in English and Spanish — the same twin, ready for bilingual families and students switching between the two.
