A guide for tutors and mentors: a mentor twin where you run your practice, and a linked mentee twin your student uses between sessions. Both speak as Eduardo.

Meet Eduardo

Eduardo — on both sides of the table

On your side, Eduardo is a collegial, direct, pedagogical assistant for running your tutoring practice. On your mentee's side, Eduardo is a strict Socratic guide scoped to the subject you mentor — never giving the answer, always guiding with questions.

The two twins

The Mentor Plan turns a once-a-week session into continuous async support with a linked pair.

TwinWho uses itWhat it's for
Mentor twinYou, the tutorDocument curriculum, track your mentee, generate reports, and read what your mentee has been working on.
Mentee twinYour studentA Socratic guide for between-session practice — scoped to the subject you mentor.

Run your practice

The mentor twin is your workspace. Here is what you do in it day to day.

1

Document curriculum & sessions

Load lesson plans, session plans, and assessments. Eduardo files them and aligns activities to curriculum standards.

2

Track your mentee

Progress shows up as events and goals — what they practiced, where they got stuck, what they've mastered.

3

Generate reports & parent recaps

Produce a Progress Report, Session Summary, or Attendance Log, and send a short recap to your mentee's parent after each session.

See your mentee's work

The mentor twin has read-only access to the mentee's workspace — between-session practice, stuck flags, and small wins. Your mentee cannot see into your workspace; the access only runs one way.

Reports & recaps

Reports are template-driven and two-phase: Eduardo finds a matching template, shows you a plan, and only generates after you approve.

ReportWhat it covers
Progress ReportAn individual mentee's events over a period.
Session / Class SummaryScores by assessment and their distribution.
Attendance LogA record of sessions attended.
Parent recapA short progress summary sent to the mentee's parent after each session.
Output is generated as PDF, Excel, or Word and saved to the Reports tab. Reports you run often can be saved as Tasks and re-run later.

What your mentee sees

The mentee twin keeps your student practicing the way you would coach them.

✓

Socratic, never the answer

Eduardo guides through questions, reframes wrong answers as "Interesting — what if…", and asks one question at a time.

✓

Between-session practice

Your mentee keeps working when you're not there; the twin is scoped to the subject you mentor.

✓

Stuck flags & a wins journal

When your mentee gets stuck it's flagged for you; small wins are journaled so you can build on them next session.

Curriculum & memory

Both twins run the education platform.

1

Import materials

Upload curriculum, assessments, and notes on the Uploads tab; Eduardo extracts and files them automatically.

2

Knowledge graph

Topics, sessions, assignments, and quiz results become linked notes, a timeline, and goals you can track.

3

It remembers

Eduardo recalls facts, preferences, and corrections across sessions.

Languages

EduAsiste works in Spanish (LATAM) and English, with support for Portuguese, French, and German. The names Eduardo and EduAsiste stay the same in every language.
